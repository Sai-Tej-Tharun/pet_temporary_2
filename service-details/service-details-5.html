A pet's ears are a warm, moist environment — the ideal breeding ground for bacteria, yeast, and ear mites if left uncleaned. Regular professional ear cleaning is one of the most effective ways to prevent the painful, expensive ear infections that affect a significant proportion of dogs and cats, particularly those with long or floppy ears.

Our ear cleaning process uses only vet-formulated ear cleaning solutions that gently dissolve wax and debris without irritating the sensitive ear canal lining. Our trained technicians perform a visual inspection of each ear before cleaning to check for redness, odour, discharge, or other signs of concern — if anything looks off, we'll alert you so you can follow up with your vet. We clean both the visible outer canal and the accessible inner canal safely, without probing too deep.

How the Session Works

01

Visual Inspection

We examine both ears visually — checking for redness, swelling, discharge, odour, or any signs that might indicate an existing infection before we begin cleaning.

02

Solution Application

A carefully measured amount of vet-approved ear cleaning solution is gently applied to the ear canal. This solution works to dissolve and loosen wax and debris safely.

03

Massage & Dislodge

We massage the base of the ear gently for 20–30 seconds to help the solution work its way through the canal and dislodge accumulated wax.

04

Wipe & Remove

Using soft, lint-free cotton pads, we carefully wipe away loosened wax and debris from the ear flap and outer canal — never probing beyond what's safely visible.

05

Post-Clean Check & Note

A final check of both ears and a brief health note is provided — we'll flag any concerns and recommend a vet visit if anything looks like it needs medical attention.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs include frequent head shaking, scratching at the ears, dark discharge, odour, redness or swelling of the ear flap or canal. Some dogs with floppy ears or those who swim regularly may need cleaning even without obvious signs. Monthly preventative cleaning is ideal for high-risk breeds like Cocker Spaniels, Basset Hounds, and Poodles.
We cannot treat active ear infections — this requires veterinary diagnosis and prescription medication. If we notice signs of an active infection during the session, we will stop and advise you to visit your vet before proceeding with cleaning. However, once treated and cleared by a vet, regular professional cleaning can help prevent future infections.
We clean the outer canal and the visible portion of the inner canal — never probing beyond what can be safely seen. Deep canal cleaning (beyond the bend of the L-shaped ear canal) requires veterinary tools and is not within the scope of grooming services. Our goal is preventative hygiene maintenance, not medical treatment.
Ear hair plucking is a topic of debate in veterinary circles. We only pluck when the hair is clearly trapping debris and moisture, creating a hygiene risk. We do not pluck routinely unless the ears show clear need. If plucking is done, we use a gentle technique and ear powder to minimise discomfort.
Yes, cats can and should have their ears checked and cleaned regularly — especially if they go outdoors or live with other pets. Ear mites are very common in cats and can spread quickly. Our approach for cats involves gentle handling, a special feline-safe cleaning solution, and patience. Most cats tolerate the process well once they're comfortable with the groomer.
